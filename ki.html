Knee Injuries

Knee injuries from earthquakes often happen due to falls or twists. They include sprains (ligament damage) and bruises to muscles or soft tissue.

First Aid Steps:

Rest the knee and avoid putting weight on it. Apply ice wrapped in cloth for 15–20 minutes every 1–2 hours for the first 48 hours to reduce swelling.

Use a compression wrap or brace for support, but not too tight. Elevate the leg above heart level to reduce swelling.

Take pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen. Start gentle movement once pain lessens, but avoid heavy activity until healed.

See a doctor if the injury is severe, you can’t walk, or the knee feels unstable or looks deformed.
